## Situation
A manuscript review initially bundled several strong credibility criticisms together, including multiple alleged inconsistencies in counts and `beta` settings.

## Action
After the author challenged the review, the critic reopened the exact cited regions in both the main text and appendix and reclassified each point.

## Outcome
Some concerns remained live, but at least one “instability” claim was acknowledged as not precisely supported by the cited line alone.

## Lesson
When a scientific critique is built from many local inconsistencies, the bundle is only as strong as its weakest citation. Re-auditing challenged items can preserve the valid methodological concerns while preventing overclaim.

## Retrieval Cues
- You are about to tell an author their empirical story is inconsistent.
- Your argument depends on several line-specific citations.
- The author asks “where exactly is the contradiction?”
