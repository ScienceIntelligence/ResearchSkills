## When + Exclusions
When writing the discussion or practical implications for a cross-sectional questionnaire thesis about family environment, educational psychology learning, and stress level.

Exclude experimental or longitudinal designs.

## Decision
Preferred: frame findings as relationships, associations, or predictive patterns.

Rejected: claiming that family environment or educational psychology learning “reduces,” “improves,” or “alleviates” stress unless the data directly support intervention effects.

Reasoning: the user’s design is cross-sectional and questionnaire-based, so causal language overstates the evidence.

## Local Verifiers
- Data collected once through questionnaires.
- No intervention group.
- No longitudinal stress measurement.
- Analysis uses correlation/regression, not causal identification.

## Failure Handling
If text already says “can reduce stress,” revise to “is associated with lower stress” or “may inform support strategies.”

## Anti-exemplars
- “Educational psychology learning alleviates stress.”
- “Family environment causes stress reduction.”
- “The study proves that improving family environment will reduce stress.”
