## When + Exclusions
When a thesis changes a hypothesis from simple association to joint prediction among family environment, educational psychology learning, and stress level.

Exclude purely wording-level edits that do not change the statistical test.

## Decision
Preferred: align the analysis plan with multiple regression or joint predictive modeling when H3 states that two predictors jointly predict stress level.

Rejected: keeping only bivariate correlation if the hypothesis uses “jointly predict.”

Reasoning: “relationship” and “joint prediction” imply different inferential analyses. The latter requires estimating the simultaneous contribution of predictors.

## Local Verifiers
- H3 uses “jointly predict.”
- The dependent variable is stress level.
- Family environment and educational psychology learning are both predictors.
- The analysis section includes regression rather than only correlation.

## Failure Handling
If the existing chapter already reports only correlations, revise H3 back to association or add a regression analysis section.

## Anti-exemplars
- Using Pearson correlation to claim joint prediction.
- Reporting separate correlations and calling them combined predictive effects.
- Changing H3 without updating objectives and research questions.
