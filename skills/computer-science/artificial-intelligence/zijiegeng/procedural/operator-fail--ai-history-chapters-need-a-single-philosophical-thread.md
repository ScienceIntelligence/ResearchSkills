## When + Exclusions
When drafting long-form AI history essays that combine mathematics, philosophy, and modern AI.

Exclude short technical explainers.

## Decision
Preferred: define one philosophical thread per chapter and make every technical detour serve that thread.

Rejected: accumulating interesting historical details without a unifying question.  
Rejected: over-explaining side concepts because they are technically interesting.

Reasoning: the user's target style is rigorous, restrained, and literary, with intellectual continuity. That style fails when the chapter becomes a pile of facts.

## Local Verifiers
- The opening question returns in the ending.
- Each section answers a sub-question.
- Technical detail changes the reader's understanding of the main idea.
- Side explanations can be removed without breaking the chapter.

## Failure Handling
If the draft sprawls, write a one-sentence thesis and delete sections that do not support it.

If it becomes too abstract, insert a concrete historical scene or technical example.

## Anti-exemplars
- A chapter that covers Hilbert, Gödel, Wittgenstein, and Turing without a shared problem.
- Ending with a summary that does not echo the opening question.
