## When + Exclusions
When writing historical chapters for readers who care about modern AI and large language models.

Exclude purely historical articles with no modern-AI ambition.

## Decision
Preferred: allow each historical idea to create a return path to modern AI, without forcing superficial analogies.

Rejected: ending each section with generic “this influenced AI today.”  
Rejected: treating modern LLMs as detached from the history of formalization, computation, and language.

Reasoning: the user's intended book differentiator is to show the deep conceptual genealogy of modern AI.

## Local Verifiers
- The modern connection follows from the chapter's core idea.
- The analogy is specific, not decorative.
- Historical limits and modern differences are both acknowledged.
- The return to modern AI appears at structurally important points.

## Failure Handling
If the modern connection feels forced, omit it or move it to the chapter conclusion.

If the historical material feels remote, identify the shared abstraction: representation, computation, learning, language, control, or agency.

## Anti-exemplars
- “This is just like ChatGPT” after every historical anecdote.
- A history chapter that never explains why modern AI researchers should care.
