## When + Exclusions
When comparing on-policy distillation with PPO-style reinforcement learning for training agents.

Exclude ordinary offline distillation from a fixed static dataset.

## Decision
Preferred: distinguish three axes: where states/prompts come from, where target actions come from, and whether the objective optimizes reward or imitates a teacher.

Rejected: describing on-policy distillation as “just PPO with a teacher.”  
Rejected: describing it as ordinary behavior cloning if rollouts are generated by the current learner.

Reasoning: on-policy distillation may collect trajectories from the learner's current distribution, then use a stronger model or policy to provide improved actions or token distributions. PPO optimizes a clipped policy-gradient objective using reward/advantage estimates.

## Local Verifiers
- Rollouts are sampled from the current learner policy.
- Teacher labels or logits are produced on those on-policy states.
- Loss is imitation-style, such as token-level KL/cross-entropy or sequence-level preference imitation.
- PPO baseline uses reward and advantage, not only teacher targets.

## Failure Handling
If the learner distribution drifts too fast, refresh teacher labels frequently or mix older trajectories.

If imitation improves format but not task success, add reward-based fine-tuning or filter teacher outputs by success.

## Anti-exemplars
- Calling static SFT “on-policy distillation.”
- Calling any teacher-student training “RL.”
