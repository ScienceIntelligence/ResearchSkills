## When + Exclusions
When revising long AI-history essays for a public but technically interested audience.

Exclude short social-media announcements.

## Decision
Preferred: preserve the core technical-philosophical idea while trimming explanatory detours that do not serve the chapter's arc.

Rejected: simplifying by removing the difficult concept entirely.  
Rejected: keeping every interesting aside for completeness.

Reasoning: the user's writing target is rigorous but readable. The right edit is compression and alignment, not conceptual dilution.

## Local Verifiers
- The main argument remains intact.
- Each paragraph advances the argument.
- Technical explanations are necessary and placed at the moment of need.
- The ending resonates with the opening problem.

## Failure Handling
If a concept is too hard, introduce it through a historical question or metaphor, then give the precise version.

If the piece feels dry, add narrative scene rather than lowering rigor.

## Anti-exemplars
- Removing Gödel/Turing-level content to make the essay “accessible.”
- Keeping long side explanations that break the narrative line.
