## When + Exclusions
When writing about information theory, cybernetics, and systems theory in the origin of AI.

Exclude purely mathematical treatments of any one of the three fields.

## Decision
Preferred: present them as three distinct but interacting lenses for understanding intelligence: information, feedback/control, and organized systems.

Rejected: collapsing them into a vague slogan such as “the three theories of informatics.”  
Rejected: treating them as a single doctrine.

Reasoning: their value for AI history is precisely that they offered different abstractions for mind, machine, communication, and regulation.

## Local Verifiers
- Information theory explains signal, uncertainty, and communication.
- Cybernetics explains feedback and control.
- Systems theory explains organization and interaction.
- The chapter shows how these lenses made intelligence engineerable.

## Failure Handling
If the section becomes too encyclopedic, connect each lens to one AI-relevant capability: representation, adaptation, or goal-directed behavior.

If the title sounds too academic, use a concrete subtitle about understanding intelligence from three angles.

## Anti-exemplars
- “Information theory, cybernetics, and systems theory are basically the same background.”
- A list of definitions with no role in the AI-origin narrative.
