## When + Exclusions
When turning an AI history essay series into a book or long-form scholarly narrative.

Exclude short popular posts whose goal is only event introduction.

## Decision
Preferred: position the work as an intellectual history of AI: mathematical logic, philosophy of language, systems thinking, computation, and modern large models as one conceptual arc.

Rejected: ordinary chronological “who invented what” AI history.  
Rejected: a purely technical textbook structure.

Reasoning: the user's differentiator is explaining the ideas behind events, not merely recounting events.

## Local Verifiers
- Each chapter has a conceptual question, not only a date or person.
- Technical content is tied to philosophical stakes.
- The narrative connects pre-AI intellectual roots to modern AI.
- The reader can see why an idea mattered beyond its historical moment.

## Failure Handling
If a chapter becomes name-heavy, rewrite around the central problem the figures were trying to solve.

If it becomes too technical, restore the historical question driving the technical detail.

## Anti-exemplars
- “From Turing to ChatGPT: a timeline.”
- A chapter that lists conferences, models, and papers without explaining the underlying conceptual shift.
