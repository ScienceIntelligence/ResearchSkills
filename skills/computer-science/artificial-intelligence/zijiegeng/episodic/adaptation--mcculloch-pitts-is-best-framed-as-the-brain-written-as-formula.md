## Situation
The user was planning an AI-history chapter on the origin of AI before Dartmouth and needed a reader-facing way to introduce the formal neuron.

## Action
Instead of presenting the McCulloch-Pitts neuron only as a historical technical model, the framing was sharpened into a conceptual question: can the human brain be written as a formula?

## Outcome
This made the neuron model function as a bridge between philosophy, logic, and connectionism, rather than as an isolated early neural-network anecdote.

## Lesson
For AI intellectual history, a technical model should be introduced through the conceptual impossibility it challenged.
