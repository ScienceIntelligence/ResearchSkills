## Fact
A non-significant control-function residual or Durbin-Wu-Hausman-style endogeneity test supports only the narrow claim that the data do not provide strong evidence against exogeneity. It does not prove exogeneity or validate OLS consistency.

## Evidence
In this session, the wording around a non-significant residual test was corrected from strong language ("confirming exogeneity" / "validates OLS") to cautious language ("no significant evidence of endogeneity was detected").

## LLM Default Belief
LLMs often overstate null results in methods writing, converting "failed to reject endogeneity-related null" into "endogeneity is absent" or "OLS is therefore correct."

## Expiry Signal
Expires if identification no longer relies on null endogeneity tests for rhetorical support, or if stronger external validity evidence makes the wording issue irrelevant.
