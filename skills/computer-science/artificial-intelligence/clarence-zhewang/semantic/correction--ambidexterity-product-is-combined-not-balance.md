## Fact
In organizational ambidexterity, a product term such as `Exploit × Explore` should be interpreted as the **combined dimension** or joint-synergy dimension, not as balance, proportion, or configuration equilibrium.

## Evidence
In this session, the manuscript originally emphasized "configuration structure" and "dual configuration," but the empirical measure was a product indicator and the reviewer pushed on the mismatch between proportion/balance language and a synergy-like variable. After reading core ambidexterity literature and addressing Reviewer 1, the conclusion was to explicitly separate:
- combined dimension: joint co-presence/synergy of exploitative and exploratory AI human capital
- balance dimension: relative evenness between the two, operationalized separately

The revision strategy was to keep the product measure as the main explanatory variable but relabel its meaning as synergy rather than balance.

## LLM Default Belief
LLMs often collapse all ambidexterity measures into a generic "balance between exploration and exploitation" story and incorrectly describe product terms as balance measures.

## Expiry Signal
If the field converges on a different standard operationalization of ambidexterity, or if the paper's focal construct is explicitly changed from combined effects to balance effects.
