## Fact
In management and information-systems research, **Digital Option Theory** does **not** mean financial binary/digital options. It refers to digitally enabled organizational capabilities that preserve future strategic flexibility, typically discussed as **knowledge options** and **process options**.

## Evidence
In this session, the first answer interpreted "digital option theory" as a finance derivative. The user explicitly corrected that reading and asked for literature-based clarification. The later discussion then stabilized on the Sambamurthy-style IS framing, including knowledge options and process options as the core conceptual forms.

## LLM Default Belief
Because "digital option" is a dominant term in finance, an LLM may confidently map the phrase to binary options unless the surrounding context explicitly signals information-systems or organizational theory.

## Expiry Signal
Expire or weaken this memory if the conversation explicitly moves into derivatives pricing, payoff structures, Black-Scholes-style valuation, or trading regulation.
