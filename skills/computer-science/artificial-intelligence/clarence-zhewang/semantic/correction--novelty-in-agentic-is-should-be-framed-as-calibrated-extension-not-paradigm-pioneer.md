## Fact
For dissertation-level theory building on human-AI collaboration, the defensible novelty may lie in a calibrated extension of existing theory and role reconceptualization, not in claiming to "transcend traditional boundaries" or "pioneer a new paradigm."

## Evidence
In this session, a strong-sounding contribution claim was explicitly diagnosed as over-elevated. The durable core that survived critique was narrower: AI should be treated as a collaborative actor for governance purposes, and the manager's role shifts toward designing interaction protocols and cognitive interventions. The successful rewrite first compressed the contribution to its irreducible claims before rebuilding only the support needed to defend those claims.

## LLM Default Belief
LLMs often default to inflating novelty language in academic contribution sections, especially around agentic AI, and overstate a theory extension as a field-defining paradigm break.

## Expiry Signal
This memory expires if the dissertation later supplies clear comparative evidence against the dominant literature showing that the stronger paradigm-level claim is genuinely warranted.
