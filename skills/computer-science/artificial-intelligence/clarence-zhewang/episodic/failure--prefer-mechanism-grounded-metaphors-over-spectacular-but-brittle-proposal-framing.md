## Situation
A generated scientific proposal used striking ideas like biological proof analogies, large financial impact claims, and precise quantitative results to sound ambitious.

## Action
The proposal was reviewed against the standard of what would make a supplementary case study convincingly better than a generic LLM-written proposal.

## Outcome
The proposal was judged likely to weaken the paper because its claims sounded ornate but not scientifically trustworthy. The failure was not lack of novelty, but low plausibility density: precise numbers, sweeping impacts, and metaphors were not anchored to a believable validation path.

## Lesson
When evaluating or drafting frontier proposals, prefer a mechanism that could realistically be tested over a concept that merely sounds visionary. A core metaphor helps only if it compresses the causal structure of the science; if it functions mainly as decoration, it reduces credibility.

## Retrieval Cues
- Proposal sounds impressive on first read but fragile on second read
- Exact percentages or monetary impacts appear without an obvious measurement path
- Metaphor is memorable, but experiment design is vague
- Reviewer reaction is “stylish, not publishable”
