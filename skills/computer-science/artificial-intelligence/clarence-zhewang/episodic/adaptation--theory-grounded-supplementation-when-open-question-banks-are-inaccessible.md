## Situation
A management-capability benchmark had clear weak subdimensions, but external multiple-choice sources were mostly paywalled, JavaScript-rendered, or otherwise unusable for direct extraction.

## Action
Instead of waiting for more scrapeable sources, the benchmark builder supplemented only the weak subdimensions with new items explicitly anchored in canonical management theories, including change management, team development, organizational culture, negotiation, and knowledge-creation frameworks.

## Outcome
The benchmark grew meaningfully while repairing coverage gaps rather than inflating already overrepresented areas. Theory-based supplementation was treated as a targeted patch for construct coverage, not a replacement for all sourced items.

## Lesson
When open-item retrieval fails, a defensible fallback is targeted item authoring tied to named canonical theories and limited to under-covered constructs. This preserves content validity better than random expansion or overreliance on whichever source happens to be accessible.

## Retrieval Cues
- Benchmark subdimensions are badly imbalanced
- External question banks are paywalled or technically inaccessible
- Need to expand a scientific evaluation dataset without drifting from the construct map
- Canonical theories exist for the missing constructs
