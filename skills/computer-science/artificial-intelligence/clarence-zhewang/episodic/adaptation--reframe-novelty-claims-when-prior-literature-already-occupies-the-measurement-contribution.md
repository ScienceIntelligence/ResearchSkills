## Situation
A dissertation initially framed one study's main innovation as the first micro-skill-based measurement paradigm for IT capability.

## Action
The claim was checked against existing literature and then rewritten to shift the contribution away from "first measurement paradigm" toward a different contribution: building an optimal digital-talent investment strategy for R&D innovation and explaining how that strategy constructs digital options.

## Outcome
The revised contribution became defensible because it no longer depended on a likely-invalid priority claim. The novelty moved from measurement primacy to theory-driven strategic insight.

## Lesson
When a "first-ever" empirical measurement claim is vulnerable, salvage the contribution by relocating novelty to the stronger layer:
- decision logic
- strategic prescription
- mechanism explanation
- theoretical integration
