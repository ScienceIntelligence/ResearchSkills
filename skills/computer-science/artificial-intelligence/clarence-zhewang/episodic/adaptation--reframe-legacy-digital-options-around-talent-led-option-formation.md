## Situation
A dissertation abstract used classic Digital Option Theory, but the focal phenomenon had shifted from direct IT investment to investment in digitally skilled people. The transition from "digital talent" to "digital options" read as conceptually abrupt.

## Action
The framing was adapted so the theory progression became:
- talent investment as the **formation pathway** of contemporary digital options,
- dispersed expertise as the **integration problem** for knowledge options,
- human-AI coordination as the **optimization problem** for process options.

## Outcome
The three studies stopped looking like separate empirical projects and instead formed a single theoretical arc: **build -> integrate -> optimize** digital options for R&D innovation.

## Lesson
When extending an older IS theory into a newer digital-economy setting, preserve the original construct but relocate its formation mechanism to the contemporary organizational carrier if the environment has changed.
