## Fact
Pelletier et al. (2016) produced a 1-km resolution regolith-thickness map bounded at 50 m maximum depth, which is the spatial anchor for defining how deep the weathered bedrock zone extends at each grid cell in a CONUS-scale Noah-MP rock moisture implementation. Without this, depth parameterization defaults to uniform assumptions that destroy spatial heterogeneity. The 50 m cap matches the physically plausible column maximum for the rock moisture module.
## Evidence
Pelletier, J.D. et al. (2016) gridded global regolith and sedimentary-deposit thickness dataset. Widely cited in CZO literature; used in Shangguan et al. (2017) regolith thickness estimates.
