## Fact
Stem water characteristic curves in mature living trees exhibit diurnal hysteresis between drying and wetting phases that mirrors soil scanning curves and can be parameterized using van Genuchten models: θ_stem = θ_r + (θ_s − θ_r)[1 + (α·|ψ|)^n]^(−m), where α and n shift as functions of VPD and soil water content (SWC). The drying path (governed by α_dry) diverges from the wetting path (governed by α_wet). Biomass hydraulic capacitance (BHC) modulates curve shape and position across species. This replaces the static P-V (pressure-volume) / cavitation curves historically derived from laboratory experiments. Reference: Cabraal, S. (2025) "Shifting the Plant Hydraulics Paradigm: Water Characteristic Curves from Mature Living Trees Show Hysteresis Mirroring Soil Physics," AMS Annual Meeting, Denver, CO.
## Evidence
KW collaborator Suvan Cabraal's field-derived data from Live Oak and Ash Juniper at White Family Outdoor Learning Center, TX. Pre-publication (AMS 2025 presentation).
