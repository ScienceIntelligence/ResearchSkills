## Fact
Primary method to spatially constrain bedrock water storage capacity (S_bedrock) across CONUS without direct bedrock hydraulic measurements: compute annual root-zone water deficit D_bedrock,Y = ΣET − ΣP − ΔS_soil over each water year. Where D_bedrock,Y > 0 repeatedly across dry years, soil storage alone cannot explain observed ET, and the accumulated deficit defines a lower bound on S_bedrock. Methodology from Dralle et al. 2020 and Hahm et al. 2022, building on McCormick et al. 2021 (Nature, "Widespread woody plant use of water stored in bedrock" — 45% of wooded land in CONUS underlain by shallow bedrock; California bedrock water use ~20 km³/yr ≈ all reservoir storage combined). Method limitations: provides lower-bound estimates only; cannot distinguish rock moisture from groundwater use; underestimates in fog/dew/lateral-flow settings; relies on remote sensing product accuracy.
## Evidence
McCormick, E.L., Dralle, D.N., Hahm, W.J. et al. Nature 597, 225–229 (2021), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-021-03761-3.
