## Fact
Long-term meteor velocity observations from the Mengcheng Meteor Radar (northern mid-latitude, China, data collected since 2014) exhibit a dual-peak velocity distribution at ~28 km/s and ~54 km/s when analyzed with a Double-Gaussian fitting approach. Major meteor showers produce 45–97% higher counts than the sporadic background and cause the upper velocity peak to amplify. Published: Wu, K., Yi, W., Xue, X., Reid, I., & Lu, M. (2024), JGR: Space Physics, 129. Implementation in MATLAB + TikZ/LaTeX visualization.
