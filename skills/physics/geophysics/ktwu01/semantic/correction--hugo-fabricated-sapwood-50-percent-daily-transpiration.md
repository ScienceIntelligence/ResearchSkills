## Fact
The commonly stated claim "trees store substantial water in sapwood — up to 50% of daily transpiration in some species" is a stylized figure used in slide decks; it is a species-specific, upper-end value (some tropical trees, some diffuse-porous species), not a universal norm. The peer-reviewed anchor is Matheny et al. (2015) showing species-specific differences in temperate forests for stem capacitance. Use the Matheny 2015 reference rather than unsourced percentages.
## LLM Default Belief
LLMs will generate "X% of daily transpiration" percentages confidently; these vary widely (10–50%+) by species, season, and methodology. Always cite the specific species and study.
