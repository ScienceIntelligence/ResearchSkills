## Fact
CONUS Noah-MP simulations at 1/8° (~12 km) resolution require approximately 500 CPU-hours per model year. The project's secured NSF NCAR allocation is 22,000 CPU-hours + 1,000 GPU-hours, sufficient for ~40+ model-year simulations per experiment including calibration ensemble runs. Adding wood moisture increases runtime ~5–10%; adding rock moisture (2–3 additional bedrock layers with Richards solution) ~15–20%; combined ~25–30% additional cost.
## Evidence
KW NCAR allocation for Noah-MP-PHS CONUS work; computational estimates from preliminary US-Syv site runs and scaling analysis.
