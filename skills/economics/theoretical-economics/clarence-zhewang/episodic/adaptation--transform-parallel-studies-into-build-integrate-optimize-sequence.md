## Situation
A dissertation contained three studies spanning different analytical levels and neighboring constructs. Examiners said the relationship among the studies was too weak and that the thesis looked like a list of separate papers.

## Action
The successful adaptation proposed in-session was to recast all three studies as addressing one focal phenomenon and connect them with a progressive sequence: construct, integrate, optimize. The editable targets were the overall framework, chapter prefaces, research-question wording, theoretical positioning, and interpretation bridges. Data and empirical findings were explicitly left untouched.

## Outcome
This produced a more defensible dissertation architecture: the work could be presented as one cumulative inquiry with internal progression, rather than as three loosely connected projects.

## Lesson
When a multi-study dissertation is criticized for weak internal logic, the first repair is not new analysis. It is often a narrative-architectural redesign that turns parallel studies into a staged knowledge contribution while preserving the empirical base.
