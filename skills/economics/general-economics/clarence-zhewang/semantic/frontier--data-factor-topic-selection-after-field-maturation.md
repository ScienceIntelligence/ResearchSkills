## Fact
For "data factors" research, the field has already moved beyond conceptual justification. The active frontier is now in measurable constructs, identification strategies, and policy evaluation, so broad "data factors affect X" ideas are likely crowded.

## Evidence
In this session, a targeted CNKI scan led to the conclusion that the literature had already reached the stage of "measurable, identifiable, policy-evaluable" work, and the user's next-step ideation was explicitly constrained to avoid duplication with that mature layer.

## Expiry Signal
This memory should be revised if new reviews show the field has shifted again, for example from identification/policy evaluation toward micro-mechanism decomposition, market design, or cross-border data governance as the dominant frontier.
